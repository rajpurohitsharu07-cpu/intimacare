Includes the penis and scrotum. The penis is responsible for sexual intercourse and urine passage. The scrotum houses the testes, maintaining optimal temperature for sperm production.

The testes produce sperm and testosterone. The epididymis stores sperm temporarily. The vas deferens transports sperm to the urethra. Seminal vesicles and prostate gland add fluids to semen for nourishment and motility.

Erectile dysfunction, low sperm count, and hormonal imbalances are some common issues. Healthy lifestyle and regular check-ups help maintain reproductive health.

Vulva, labia, clitoris, and vaginal opening form the external reproductive organs. The clitoris is highly sensitive and plays a major role in sexual arousal.

The ovaries produce eggs and hormones (estrogen, progesterone). Fallopian tubes transport eggs to the uterus. The uterus supports pregnancy. The cervix connects uterus and vagina, and regulates menstrual flow.

Hormonal fluctuations influence menstrual cycle, sexual desire, mood, and fertility. Irregularities can affect libido and reproductive health.
