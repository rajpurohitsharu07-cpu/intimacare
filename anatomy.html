Bodies & Anatomy

Understand reproductive anatomy, sexual response, hormones, and common concerns with clear illustrations.

The male reproductive system consists of external and internal structures, including the penis, testes, epididymis, vas deferens, seminal vesicles, and prostate gland. Proper understanding is essential for sexual and reproductive health.

The female reproductive system includes the ovaries, fallopian tubes, uterus, cervix, and vagina. Hormonal balance and anatomy knowledge are key for menstrual health and sexual well-being.

The sexual response cycle includes four phases: excitement, plateau, orgasm, and resolution. Awareness of these stages helps understand normal sexual function and identify potential concerns.

Key hormones like testosterone, estrogen, and progesterone influence libido, sexual function, and mood. Imbalances may lead to reduced sexual desire or other issues.

Common sexual health concerns include erectile difficulties, low libido, premature ejaculation, irregular periods, and pain during intercourse. Understanding anatomy and physiology helps in identifying possible causes and solutions.
